About the Courseer

Built by Sayan Patra as a public modelling notebook

I am a data science and machine learning student with a background in mathematics. My work is shaped by a simple belief: models should not be treated as black boxes before we understand the structure underneath them.

This website is my attempt to turn machine learning study into a research habit. Each section is written like a compact investigation: define the problem, derive the model, inspect the assumptions, test the failure modes, and connect the baseline to larger systems.

The Motive

Many students learn machine learning as a sequence of tools. This site treats it as a sequence of research questions. What is the model assuming? What does the objective optimize? Where does inference break? What does the residual structure reveal?

  • Start with the modelling question.
  • Derive the estimator before using the library call.
  • Connect equations with diagnostics, code, and case studies.
  • Track assumptions, residuals, error, and generalization.
  • Use classical models as the research spine for modern ML.
